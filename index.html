üöÄ Quick Start Guide (For Your First Game!)

Welcome to Starfall! This guide will get you playing quickly.

üèÜ 1. What's the Goal?

Be the first to either:

  • Build 3 Complete Solar Systems in your Star Chart (the area in front of you).
  • Play all 3 special "Supernova" cards from your hand at once.

Îç± 2. What's in the Deck?

  • Star Cards: üåü Big suns! You play these first. They say how many "Discoveries" they need.
  • Discovery Cards: ü™ê Planets, comets, etc. You attach these to your Stars.
  • Miracle Cards: ‚ú® Good for you! One-time powerful effects. (The 3 Supernovas are special Miracle cards for winning).
  • Enhancement Cards: üîó Special cards that attach to things in your Star Chart for lasting effects!
  • Disaster Cards: üí• Bad for your opponents! One-time disruptive effects.

üõ†Ô∏è 3. Setting Up

  • Shuffle the big deck of cards.
  • Deal 5 cards to each player.
  • Player to the dealer's left goes first, then play goes clockwise.

üéÆ 4. Your Turn (The Basics)

Each turn, you do these things in order:

  1. Draw: Take 1 card from the deck.
  2. Action Phase (Playing Cards):
    • Can I Play? If you have 6 or more cards in your hand, you can start playing cards!
    • What Can I Do? You can keep playing cards from your hand (Stars, Discoveries, Miracles, Enhancements, Disasters, or even whole Solar Systems) one by one, as long as you still have 6 or more cards before you play each card.
    • Once you drop to 5 cards (or fewer) in hand after playing a card, your Action Phase for playing cards from hand ends.
    • If you start your Action Phase with 5 or fewer cards (after drawing), you cannot play any cards from your hand this turn.
  3. End Your Turn:
    • Hand Size Check (Again): You should already be at 5 or fewer cards if you played any. If you didn't play any cards and have more than 5, discard extras to the bottom of the deck until you have 5.
    • Check for Win: Did you meet a win condition? If yes, you win!
    • Pass the turn to your left.

‚òÄÔ∏è 5. Building a Solar System

  • A Star card has a "Class" number (e.g., Class 3). This means it needs that many Discovery cards.
  • You play Stars and attach Discoveries to them in your Star Chart.
  • Once a Star has the right number of Discoveries, its Solar System is "Complete"!

‚õìÔ∏è 6. Playing Cards Off-Turn (Reactions/Chains)

Even if it's not your turn, if someone plays a card, you might be able to play a Miracle, Disaster, or even an Enhancement card from your hand to react! This is called a Chain. Ask experienced players for details!

(Once you've played a game or two, read the Detailed Rules for how "Chains" work and the specifics of winning.)

üìñ Starfall: Detailed Rulebook

üéØ 1. Game Objective

Be the first player to achieve one of two victory conditions:

  • Astronomical Domination: Control three complete Solar Systems in your Star Chart at the end of your turn.
  • Cosmic Cataclysm: Successfully play all three unique "Supernova" Miracle cards (Fusion, Gravity, Gas) from your hand. This can be done during your Action Phase or as a reaction in a Chain.

üîë 2. Components & Key Terms

  • Main Deck: The shared deck of cards (currently 90 cards).
  • Card Types:
    • Star Cards: üåü The foundation of a Solar System. Each has a Class value, which determines how many Discovery cards are needed to complete its system. Played into your Star Chart.
    • Discovery Cards: ü™ê Represent planets, comets, asteroids. Attached to Star cards in a Star Chart. Cannot be played by themselves.
    • Miracle Cards: ‚ú® Powerful, one-time effects. Resolve after any Chain, then placed bottom of Main Deck.
      • Supernova Cards (Fusion, Gravity, Gas): Unique Miracle cards for the Cosmic Cataclysm victory.
    • Enhancement Cards: üîó Special cards that attach to other cards (usually in a Star Chart) to provide persistent effects or abilities. Subject to specific play requirements on the card. May be played into a Chain.
    • Disaster Cards: üí• Powerful, one-time disruptive effects. Resolve after any Chain, then placed bottom of Main Deck.
  • Star Chart: Your face-up play area for Stars, attached Discoveries, Enhancements, and completed Solar Systems.
  • Hand: Private cards held. End-of-turn limit is 5.
  • Destroy: Card goes to the bottom of the Main Deck from Star Chart or hand (if specified).
  • Complete a Solar System: 1 Star Card + Discovery Cards equal to Star's "Class" in a Star Chart.
  • Chain: Sequence of reactively played Miracle, Disaster, or Enhancement cards, resolving in reverse (LIFO).
  • Chain Link (CL): Each card in a Chain. CL1 is the initial play being responded to.

‚öôÔ∏è 3. Game Setup

  1. Shuffle the Main Deck and place it face down.
  2. Choose a dealer. Player to dealer's left is first.
  3. Deal 5 cards to each player.

üîÑ 4. Player Turn Sequence

Play proceeds clockwise. A turn has three phases:

  • Phase 1: Start & Draw Phase (Automatic)
    1. At turn start, draw 1 card from Main Deck.
    2. Check for start-of-turn effects (e.g., rotation tracking).
  • Phase 2: Action Phase (Playing Cards from Hand)
    1. Check Hand Size: After drawing, must have 6+ cards to play cards. If 5 or fewer, skip to End Phase.
    2. Playing Multiple Cards: If 6+ cards, you may make one "play" (a card or predefined group).
      • After this play (and any Chain) resolves, if still 6+ cards, you may make another play.
      • Continue making plays one-by-one while 6+ cards before each play.
      • If a play drops hand to 5 or fewer, no more plays this Action Phase.
    3. Each play may start a Chain (Sec 5). Played card(s) resolve after any Chain.
  • Phase 3: End Phase
    1. Discard (if needed): If >5 cards, discard to 5. Discards go to bottom of Main Deck.
    2. Check for Victory: If a victory condition met, announce it. You win!
    3. Check for end-of-turn effects (e.g., rotation tracking).
    4. Turn ends. Play passes left.

üÉè 5. Playing Cards & The Chain

  • General Play from Hand (During Your Action Phase):

    If 6+ card hand size met, a "play" can be:

    • Playing a Star Card: Place into your Star Chart.
    • Playing a Discovery Card: Attach to an incomplete Star in your Star Chart.
    • Playing a Miracle, Enhancement, or Disaster Card: Announce it. (Enhancements typically attach per their text).
    • Playing a Complete Solar System: Play 1 Star + required Discoveries from hand into Star Chart.
    • Playing Supernova Miracle Cards for Victory: If you hold all three, play simultaneously. If successful, you win. Meets Cosmic Cataclysm.
  • The Chain and Reactive Play (Miracle, Disaster, & Enhancement Cards ONLY):
    • Starting a Chain: Active player's play (CL1) doesn't resolve immediately. Opportunity for reactions.
    • Responding: Opponents (in turn order, left of active player first) may play one Miracle card, one Disaster card, or one Enhancement card from their hand to respond. This is CL2, etc.
      • Only Miracle, Disaster, or Enhancement cards may be played into a Chain. Star cards, Discovery cards, or groups like "Complete Solar Systems" cannot be played reactively.
      • Hand size (6-card rule) does not restrict reactive plays into a Chain.
      • Enhancement cards played into a Chain must still meet their play/attachment requirements upon resolution. If their target is invalid, they may fail to attach and be discarded.
    • Continuing the Chain: Priority passes for more reactions until all pass consecutively.
    • Resolving the Chain: Resolves LIFO (last card played resolves first, down to CL1).
    • Important: No new plays/Chains while a Chain is resolving.
    • Card Destination: Miracles and Disasters played in a Chain go to bottom of Main Deck after resolving. Enhancements attach to their target if valid upon resolution; otherwise, they are also placed on the bottom of the Main Deck.
    • Impact on Hand Size: Reactive plays reduce hand size, possibly affecting your next Action Phase if hand drops below 6 after your draw. You don't "miss turns" (skip phases).

‚ú® 6. Special Rules & Card Effects

  • Tracking Limited-Duration Effects (e.g., an effect lasting "for two turns"):

    Use the card (or token) as a reminder near affected player/cards.

    1. Activation: Effect resolves, reminder upright. Effect active.
    2. End of Affected Entity's 1st Full Turn After Activation: Rotate reminder 90¬∞. Effect continues.
    3. End of Affected Entity's 2nd Full Turn After Activation: Effect expires. Remove reminder (e.g., card to bottom of Main Deck).
